The Little Village of Denton, Nebraska

May 16th, 2017

“Bloody rain, when does it stop?”

The town is quiet, too quiet, perhaps. One might think, “Well it’s midday, someone’s gotta rest.” Still, no one is to be found, except for us and the birds.

“Where are they?”

The swings at the play ground sway elegantly in the spring breeze. The sky continue to water our heads, gallons upon gallons.

“Where are they?”

Despite the warm rain, birds continue to chirp, joining the rain in the grand symphony of nature–a sad one, perhaps, but grand. Denton, then, is a jungle surrounded by fields of emptiness.

“Are they, the residents, just intoxicated by the silence and the alluring beauty that the trees around them seem too happy to provide?”
